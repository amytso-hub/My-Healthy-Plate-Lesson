Think–Pair–Share

Why do Fruit & Vegetables occupy the largest section?

Fruit and vegetables provide dietary fibre, vitamins, minerals and protective plant compounds. Making them the largest part of the meal helps create a more nutrient-rich and balanced plate.

Recommend at least two improvements

Suggested improvements

  • Add more vegetables: a few cucumber slices are not enough to make up half the plate.
  • Choose brown rice: this increases wholegrain intake and provides more fibre than white rice.
  • Choose skinless chicken: removing the skin reduces the fat content of the meal.
  • Replace the sweetened drink with water: this reduces added sugar and supports hydration.
Complete all four judgements and write two specific recommendations.
👩‍🏫 Teacher Prompt
Ask students to justify each judgement using the plate proportions, food choice, preparation method and drink guideline.

Exit Ticket

Complete the final three questions independently.

5 minutes
1. Which group should occupy half the plate?
2. Which statement best follows the cooking-oil guideline?
3. What is the best improvement to the chicken rice set?
1. Fruit & Vegetables

They occupy half the plate because they provide fibre, vitamins, minerals and protective plant compounds.

2. Use healthier oils in moderation.

The type and amount of oil both matter. Healthier oils should still be used in sensible amounts.

3. Add more vegetables, choose brown rice and replace the drink with water.

This improves the plate proportions, increases wholegrain intake and reduces added sugar.

Answer all three questions.
